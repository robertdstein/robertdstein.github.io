About Me

Since July 2017 I've been a PhD student at DESY Zeuthen, supervised by Anna Franckowiack. I grew up in London, and did my undergraduate studies there at Imperial College. I completed my masters project in the Astroparticle group of the University of Hamburg, on the topic of Reconstruction of Heavy Cosmic rays using Cherenkov Light. Now I've moved into high-energy neutrino physics and multi-messenger astronomy, as a member of the Icecube and ZTF collaborations. My research interests include:

  • IceCube Neutrino Alerts: When IceCube detects high-energy neutrinos, it publishes alerts to publically notify astronomers around the world.
  • Neutrino Follow-up: We don't know exactly where high-energy neutrinos come from, but by observing the neutrino arrival directions with telescopes, we might be able to find out. With ZTF, I have led many of our neutrino follow-up campaigns.
  • Gravitational-Wave (GW) + Gamma-Ray Burst (GRB) follow-up:
    • Observations of the first Binary Neutron-Star merger from LIGO revealed a spectacular kilonova AT2017gfo, detected with a variety of telescopes. With ZTF we search for these kilonovae from the direction of GW/GRB triggers.
  • IceCube multi-messenger correlation studies: Analyses can identify correlations between neutrinos and astrophysical catologues. I performed the first IceCube correlation study with Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs).
